Atlanta EIDs Volunteer at

Habitat For Humanity

Last Saturday, nine Atlanta EIDs volunteered at the local Habitat for Humanity and helped construct affordable housing for the community. This experience gave interns a hands-on experience working on home improvement projects and provided a great team building opportunity.

If you'd like to help your local Habitat for Humanity, visit www.habitat.org/!

Exploring the LP's: OMLP

My name is Stephanie Keller, I am currently a 5th year senior at the Ohio State University. This coming winter I will be graduating with a degree in Industrial and Systems Engineering and a minor in Computer and Information Science. Currently, I am interning in the Service Center, focusing on machine guarding and applying lean and six sigma concepts to the shop floor. I am heading the machine guarding initiative to improve safety of the operators using common metal cutting machines.

As an (Operations Management Leadership Program) OMLP intern, we work on everything that comes into or out of the shop, including procuring raw materials with sourcing, manufacturing new products by the standard operating procedures, repairing products from the field, selling products ready for market, and ensuring the quality constraints set by the design engineers.

- Stephanie Keller (Atlanta, GA)
